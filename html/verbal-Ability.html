Synonyms

Synonyms are words with the same or very similar meanings. Mastering them is crucial for vocabulary-based aptitude questions.

Trick: Look for root words and prefixes/suffixes to decipher meaning. Consider the context. Eliminate options you know are incorrect.

Question: Choose the synonym for "Sagacious."

Options: (a) Foolish (b) Wise (c) Ignorant (d) Naive

Answer: (b) Wise. "Sagacious" means wise or shrewd.

Commonly Tested Synonyms:

  • Astute
  • Perspicacious
  • Erudite
  • Sagacious
  • Profound

Antonyms

Antonyms are words that have opposite meanings. Just like synonyms, they are important for building vocabulary and are frequently tested.

Trick: Think of the core meaning and its direct opposite. Look for prefixes like "un-," "dis-," "in-," and "non-." Consider the context.

Question: Choose the antonym for "Benevolent."

Options: (a) Kind (b) Generous (c) Malevolent (d) Compassionate

Answer: (c) Malevolent. "Benevolent" means kind and generous, while "malevolent" means having or showing a wish to do evil to others.

Commonly Tested Antonyms:

  • Optimistic
  • Pessimistic
  • Abundant
  • Scarce
  • Transient
  • Permanent

Prepositions

Prepositions show the relationship between a noun or pronoun and other words in a sentence. They often indicate location, time, or direction.

Trick: Visualize the relationship. Familiarize yourself with common prepositions. Context is key.

Question: The cat jumped ____ the chair.

Options: (a) at (b) on (c) in (d) under

Answer: (b) on. This indicates the cat jumped onto the surface of the chair.

Commonly Confused Prepositions:

  • in / on
  • at / to
  • during / for
  • since / for
  • among / between
