Symbols and Notations Reasoning

Understanding symbols and notations is crucial for logical reasoning questions in competitive exams. Below is a guide with common symbols, meanings, tricks, and examples.

Common Symbols and Their Meanings

Symbol Meaning Example
> Greater than 5 > 3 (True)
< Less than 2 < 6 (True)
≥ Greater than or equal to 4 ≥ 4 (True)
≤ Less than or equal to 3 ≤ 5 (True)
= Equal to 7 = 7 (True)
≠ Not equal to 8 ≠ 5 (True)

Tricks to Solve Symbol-Based Questions

Follow these tips to solve symbol and notation-based reasoning questions quickly:

  • Understand the relationship: Symbols usually represent mathematical or logical relationships. Identify them correctly.
  • Use substitution: Replace the symbols with their actual meaning and check if the statement makes sense.
  • Follow step-by-step analysis: Break down complex expressions into smaller parts.
  • Practice pattern recognition: Some questions follow repetitive patterns. Recognizing them saves time.

Example Problem

Question: If A @ B means A is greater than B, A # B means A is less than B, and A $ B means A is equal to B, then which of the following is true?

(a) 5 @ 3

(b) 2 # 6

(c) 4 $ 4

Solution:

Using the given symbols, we interpret:

  • 5 @ 3 → 5 > 3 (True)
  • 2 # 6 → 2 < 6 (True)
  • 4 $ 4 → 4 = 4 (True)

Thus, all given options are correct.
